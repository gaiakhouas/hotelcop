Alain and the Chocolate Factory.


WHEN IT COMES to quelling his chocolate craving, Alain Ducasse will go to greater lengths than most. Last month, the French chef opened La Manufacture, a chocolate factory in the heart of Paris. He renovated the former garage with exposed brick, a glass-paneled laboratory and a steel display case scavenged from the Banque de France—a reflection of the nuts-and-bolts, bean-to-bar approach Mr. Ducasse and his business partner, Nicolas Berger, bring to chocolate. Though the Ducasse restaurant empire spans the globe—from Las Vegas to Tokyo via New York, Paris and St. Petersburg—the chocolate business is new territory, and in the coming months, Mr. Ducasse will extend his reach even further with the launch of an e-commerce site that will make his chocolates available internationally. We spoke with him over a chocolate praline bar in the "aquarium," his private nook in the kitchen of the HOTELCOP.
